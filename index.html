1. Core Metric Impact Analysis (What Changes What)

Below is a complete breakdown of every number that changes when you perform a specific task, based on the game's code:

1. User Acquisition (UA) Task

Action: You increase the traffic to your startup.

Formulas & Impacts:

  • Scaling Factor = New UA / Old UA
  • Revenue = Old Revenue √ó Scaling Factor
  • Cost of Sales = Old Cost of Sales √ó Scaling Factor
  • Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) = Old CPA √ó Scaling Factor
  • Marketing Budget = Old Marketing √ó Scaling Factor
  • Buyer Count = floor((New UA √ó Conversion) / 100)
  • ARPU = CLTV √ó (Conversion / 100)
  • Contribution Margin = New UA √ó (ARPU - CPA)

Summary: Increasing traffic scales up almost everything‚ÄîRevenue, Costs, and Marketing Budget all multiply by the growth factor.

2. Conversion First Purchase (C1) Task

Action: You improve the percentage of visitors who buy.

Formulas & Impacts:

  • Buyer Count = UA √ó (New C1 / 100)
  • ARPU = CLTV √ó (New C1 / 100)
  • Revenue = Buyer Count √ó APC √ó AOV
  • Cost of Sales = Buyer Count √ó APC √ó COGS
  • Contribution Margin = UA √ó (ARPU - CPA)

Summary: A higher C1 directly increases Buyers and ARPU, which then recalculates total Revenue and Costs based on the new buyer volume.

3. Average Order Value (AOV) Task

Action: You increase the price of your product.

Formulas & Impacts:

  • New C1 = Old C1 √ó 0.90 (Conversion drops 10%)
  • Buyer Count = UA √ó (New C1 / 100)
  • Revenue = Buyer Count √ó APC √ó New AOV
  • Cost of Sales = Buyer Count √ó APC √ó COGS
  • Marketing = UA √ó CPA

Summary: Raising prices boosts Revenue per sale but automatically lowers your Conversion Rate (C1) by 10%, reducing your Buyer Count.

4. Average Payment Count (APC) Task

Action: You get customers to purchase more frequently.

Formulas & Impacts:

  • Total Orders = (UA √ó Conversion / 100) √ó New APC
  • Revenue = AOV √ó Total Orders
  • Cost of Sales = COGS √ó Total Orders
  • CLTV = (AOV - COGS) √ó New APC
  • ARPU = CLTV √ó (Conversion / 100)
  • Contribution Margin = UA √ó (ARPU - CPA)

Summary: Increasing repeat purchases (APC) drives up Total Orders, which significantly increases Revenue, Cost of Sales, and Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV).

5. Cost Per Acquisition (CPA) Task

Action: You change how much you spend to get a user (Efficiency vs. Volume trade-off).

Logic Check (Efficiency vs. Aggression):

  • If New CPA < Old CPA: UA = floor(UA √ó 0.8) (UA drops 20%)
  • If New CPA > Old CPA: UA = floor(UA √ó 1.2) (UA grows 20%)

Formulas & Impacts:

  • New CPA = Target CPA
  • Buyer Count = floor((New UA √ó Conversion) / 100)
  • Total Orders = Buyer Count √ó APC
  • Revenue = AOV √ó Total Orders
  • Cost of Sales = COGS √ó Total Orders
  • Marketing = New UA √ó New CPA
  • Contribution Margin = New UA √ó (ARPU - New CPA)

Summary: Lowering CPA makes you efficient but reduces traffic (UA drops 20%). Increasing CPA gets you more traffic (UA grows 20%) but costs more per user.

6. Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) Task

Action: You change the cost to produce your product (Improving margins).

Formulas & Impacts:

  • Total Orders = (UA √ó Conversion / 100) √ó APC
  • Cost of Sales = Total Orders √ó New COGS
  • CLTV = (AOV - New COGS) √ó APC
  • ARPU = CLTV √ó (Conversion / 100)
  • Contribution Margin = UA √ó (ARPU - CPA)

Summary: Lowering COGS directly improves your profit margins. It updates Cost of Sales, CLTV, ARPU, and Contribution Margin without affecting Revenue or UA.

2. Strategic Execution Roadmap

Buyer Calculation Logic

For the FFF stage, our immediate goal is 10 buyers.

Buyer = Ua √ó (C1 / 100)

(This represents C1% of Ua)

Strategic Insight: Performing tasks that improve C1 (Conversion Rate) and Ua (User Acquisition) will directly increase the total number of buyers.

Stage Progression & Strategy

FFF Stage: Effectively an introduction.

Angels Stage: The real game begins. The goal is 100 buyers. You must take charge of critical decisions.

Warning: Financial pressure increases significantly with each stage. Rent, salaries, and hiring costs rise, increasing the "turn amount" needed to sustain operations.

The Growth Loop (Repeat Every Stage)

To efficiently advance through Angels and beyond, repeat this cycle:

  1. 1
    Perform CPA Tasks First: This lowers your acquisition cost. It may decrease UA temporarily, but it improves long-term efficiency (lowering the cash required for future growth).
  2. 2
    Perform UA & C1 Tasks Second: Once efficiency is set, scale up using User Acquisition and Conversion tasks to hit buyer targets.
  3. 3
    Monitor Cash: If cash reserves dip too low, seek investment immediately.
